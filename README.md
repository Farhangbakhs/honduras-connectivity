# Honduras School Connectivity: MILP Optimisation

Interactive visualisation and output data for a **Mixed-Integer Linear Programme (MILP)** that maximises internet connectivity across Honduran public schools over a five-year planning horizon.

---

## Files

### `DeterministicMap.html`
A self-contained interactive dashboard built with [MapLibre GL](https://maplibre-gl-js.com/). Open it in any modern browser with no server required. On load it prompts you to upload the four CSV files below, then renders a deployment timeline across Honduras showing which schools get connected each year, which towers are built or equipped with WISP, and headline connectivity statistics. Supports two model variants (with / without annual milestones) and a year-by-year playback mode.

### CSV outputs

| File | Description |
|---|---|
| `MilestonesSchools.csv` | School-level results for the **Milestone-Constrained** model. Fields: `id`, `name`, `lat`, `lon`, `dept`, `municipio`, `connected`, `year`, `had_dev`, `got_dev`. |
| `MilestonesSites.csv` | Tower/WISP site results for the **Milestone-Constrained** model. Fields: `site_id`, `lat`, `lon`, `is_existing`, `got_tower`, `got_wisp`, `tower_year`, `wisp_year`. |
| `NoMilestoneSchools.csv` | School-level results for the **Unrestricted Efficiency** model (no annual targets). Same schema as above. |
| `NoMilestoneSites.csv` | Tower/WISP site results for the **Unrestricted Efficiency** model. Same schema as above. |

## Model overview

A school is *connected* when it holds a digital device package **and** lies within 10 km of a WISP-equipped tower. Investment follows a three-layer prerequisite chain (tower > WISP > devices) spread across 18 Honduran departments with a shared $125M budget over five years.

Two objectives are compared:

- **Unrestricted Efficiency** maximises total year-5 connected schools with no intermediate targets, giving the solver full flexibility over investment timing (upper bound).
- **Milestone-Constrained Efficiency** applies the same objective but penalises shortfalls against annual connectivity targets (10% to 30% of unconnected schools), reflecting the political requirement for visible year-on-year progress.

---

## Key findings

**Unrestricted efficiency (no milestones).** The model connects **12,409 of 12,578 schools (98.7%)** by year 5, deploying 10,705 device packages, building 330 new towers, and installing 1,336 WISP units within the $125M budget. The solver concentrates 94% of WISP installations and 99.4% of connections in year 5, avoiding four years of recurring OPEX ($3,820 per school per year) and freeing approximately $15,280 per school for additional CAPEX. Six departments (Lempira, Santa Barbara, Cortes, Copan, Intibuca, Atlantida) reach 100% connectivity. The 169 unreached schools (1.3%) lie outside any candidate tower's 10 km WISP radius.

**Milestone-constrained efficiency.** Imposing annual deployment targets reduces year-5 connectivity to **9,363 schools (74.4%)**, a gap of 3,046 schools relative to the unconstrained plan despite identical budget. Early years now account for 31% of total connections (versus 0.6% without milestones) as WISP installations begin in year 1. The recurring OPEX generated by early connections crowds out later CAPEX, explaining the 24.3 percentage-point shortfall.

**Cost of gradual deployment.** Each percentage-point gain in annual milestone compliance costs approximately 250 fewer schools connected by year 5, quantifying the trade-off between political visibility and infrastructure efficiency.

## How to use the map

1. Open `DeterministicMap.html` in Chrome, Firefox, or Edge.
2. Upload the four CSV files when prompted (drag-and-drop or click to browse).
3. Click **Launch Map**.
4. Use the mode pills in the header to switch between model variants, and the timeline bar at the bottom to step through years 1 to 5.
